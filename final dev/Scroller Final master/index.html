Heatwaves are happening more often


Deadly heat waves have started occurring with disturbing frequency and duration
due to global warming and an increasingly unforgiving urban heat island. These are
the longest heatwaves in NYC's history since 1895.

Source: Adapt NY

It can be hotter inside during a heat wave


This unique study tracked indoor temperature within a 7th floor southeast-facing apartment in Harlem. People often believe they're taking refuge from extreme temperatures by staying indoors but this is not always the case. Even when it cools down at night, building materials can trap the heat inside.

Source: Harlem Heat Project

12.5% of NYC Seniors Lack AC at Home


The city does provide free air conditioning to at-risk seniors but many don't want
to assume the utility bills. And even though cooling centers are available throughout
the city, this low-mobility population is more inclined to stay at home.

Source: NYC Environment and Health Data Portal (2013)











Learning from Chicago


The 1995 Chicago heat wave is equally known for its high death toll and poor response
by city officials. Over 600 people died, and the iconic images of refrigerated mobile
morgues brought in to handle the overflow of bodies seemingly shamed city officials into
overhauling their heat response plan.

A similar heat wave in 1999 yielded a far lower death toll. This was primarly due to the city
providing free and direct transportation to cooling centers and having police officers go
door-to-door, checking on seniors and dispatching EMTs when necessary.

NYC currently does not include these practices in their heat response plan.
